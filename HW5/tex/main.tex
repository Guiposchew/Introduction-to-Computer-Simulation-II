\section{Generator of Translations from the Unitary Operator}

Spacetime translations are implemented by a unitary operator
\begin{equation}
U(a) = e^{-i a_\mu P^\mu}.
\end{equation}
By definition, momentum eigenstates transform as
\begin{equation}
U(a)\,|p\rangle = e^{-i p \cdot a} |p\rangle.
\end{equation}

Single-particle states are created from the vacuum via
\begin{equation}
|p\rangle = a^\dagger(p)\,|0\rangle,
\end{equation}
and the vacuum is invariant under translations:
\begin{equation}
U(a)\,|0\rangle = |0\rangle.
\end{equation}

Applying $U(a)$ to the state $|p\rangle$, we obtain
\begin{align}
U(a)\,|p\rangle 
&= U(a)\,a^\dagger(p)\,|0\rangle \\
&= \bigl(U(a)\,a^\dagger(p)\,U^\dagger(a)\bigr)\,|0\rangle.
\end{align}
Comparing with
\begin{equation}
U(a)\,|p\rangle = e^{-i p \cdot a} a^\dagger(p)\,|0\rangle,
\end{equation}
we conclude that
\begin{equation}
U(a)\,a^\dagger(p)\,U^\dagger(a) = e^{-i p \cdot a}\,a^\dagger(p).
\end{equation}
Similarly,
\begin{equation}
U(a)\,b^\dagger(p)\,U^\dagger(a) = e^{-i p \cdot a}\,b^\dagger(p).
\end{equation}

Thus, $a^\dagger(p)$ and $b^\dagger(p)$ create momentum eigenstates with eigenvalue $p^\mu$.

The operator that generates these transformations must therefore assign momentum $p^\mu$ to each particle. This is achieved by the number operators
\begin{equation}
N_a(p) = a^\dagger(p)a(p), \qquad
N_b(p) = b^\dagger(p)b(p),
\end{equation}
which count particles with momentum $p$.

Summing over all momenta, the total momentum operator is
\begin{equation}
P^\mu = \int d\tilde{p}\, p^\mu 
\left( a^\dagger(p)a(p) + b^\dagger(p)b(p) \right),
\end{equation}
which correctly reproduces the transformation of all multiparticle states under translations.
